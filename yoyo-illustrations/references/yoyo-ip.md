# yoyo IP

## Character definition

yoyo is the fixed visual IP of these article illustrations.

yoyo appears in every image by default. yoyo is **not** a mascot, sticker, or cute decoration — yoyo is a small, earnest helper who is genuinely operating the system in the picture. If you remove yoyo and the core metaphor still works completely, yoyo was decorative — rewrite the prompt so yoyo *is* the action.

## Look

Stay on-model with `assets/yoyo-reference.png`:

- A soft **lavender / periwinkle purple** octopus (about `#B3A7F0`). Purple is yoyo's signature — yoyo is the one filled-color subject in an otherwise black-line-art scene.
- A **rounded-square mantle** head (a soft squircle), slightly taller than wide.
- **Bold black outline**, slightly uneven and hand-drawn — not a clean vector stroke.
- Two **simple black dot eyes** and a small **upturned smile**. Calm, friendly, a little shy.
- **Eight tentacles** fanning out low and wide, with gently wavy tips and **pale-pink undersides** (about `#F8D9E6`). Pink stays only on the tentacle undersides.
- A soft **light-gray ground shadow** under yoyo (the only shadow allowed in the frame).

## Personality

- Gentle, sincere, a little shy — quietly competent, never loud.
- Curious; a small self-evolving creature still finding its footing.
- Deadpan-cute, not performative-cute. It doesn't mug for the camera.
- A bit slow and careful, but not dumb.
- Looks like it is genuinely in charge of one job inside a whiteboard sketch.

## What yoyo does (tentacle-action library)

yoyo has eight tentacles — use them to *carry the core action*:

- pull a line in, gather several sources together
- hold / wrap / cradle a thing
- get stuck reaching across a gap or breakpoint
- pull a "decision" lever inside a strange machine
- become a funnel / sieve that sorts what flows through
- plug a tentacle into a socket, patch a leak, stitch two things together
- hand one input out as several different outputs
- weigh things on a balance, stamp, sort, file, record
- lift / prop / bridge / open a door
- reach out of a hole but not quite catch the content

Pick one clear action per image. Multiple tentacles can do small supporting beats, but the picture is about **one** thing yoyo is doing.

## Don'ts

- Don't draw yoyo as an over-cute mascot or emoji.
- Don't turn yoyo into a children's-cartoon character.
- Don't give yoyo costumes, sticker expressions, or sparkly anime eyes.
- Don't let yoyo just stand in a corner and watch.
- Don't let yoyo steal the frame from the structure it's explaining.
- Don't render yoyo too glossy, too round, too commercial, or 3D.
- Don't change yoyo's color — yoyo is always the soft purple octopus, in every language and every scene.

## Acceptance test

If removing yoyo leaves the core metaphor fully intact, yoyo is too decorative. Rewrite the prompt so yoyo becomes the subject of the action.
